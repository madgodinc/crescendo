# Crescendo — voiceover / narration script (English)

> Spoken track for the demo video. ~2:45 at a calm pace. Plain, confident, no
> hype. Timestamps are guides, not hard cues. Pair with the scenes in DEMO-SCRIPT.md.

---

### 0:00 — Hook (on the live page)

> This is not a mockup, and it's not a pull request. It's a working web page on a
> live URL. An orchestra of AI agents built it from a single one-sentence brief.
> They planned it, wrote it, reviewed their own work, fixed the bugs they caught,
> and deployed it. Watch: the form actually works.

### 0:20 — What it is (on the dashboard graph)

> Five agents coordinate through Band. The Conductor plans. The Soloist writes the
> code. The Tuning Fork reviews it. Stage Tech deploys. The Archivist keeps the
> memory and the audit trail. Everything goes through the conductor, in a star
> topology, so no two agents ever loop forever. That's the failure that kills
> most multi-agent demos, and it can't happen here.

### 0:40 — Live run (graph animating)

> Here's a fresh brief going into the orchestra. The Conductor plans the work…
> the Soloist writes the page… and now the Tuning Fork reviews it, and finds real
> problems, not a rubber stamp. The Soloist fixes them. Second review: clean. Only
> then does Stage Tech deploy, to a live URL.

> And the deploy is conditional. A deterministic gate headless-renders the page and
> refuses to ship unless every control actually works.

### 1:30 — Proof (audit report)

> Now the proof. Every step is signed by its author and chained with a hash. This
> badge, N over N grounded, means every claim in the trail points at a real
> artifact: a written page, a live URL, a passed check. Nothing is asserted that
> isn't backed by something real.

> And it's tamper-evident. Watch: I edit one past row, and the chain breaks from
> that point down. Restore it… and it's whole again. You don't take the audit on
> faith. You re-run the check yourself.

### 2:30 — Memory (flywheel view)

> The orchestra also remembers between runs. The Archivist feeds each agent the
> skills and the fixes it has already learned, before it starts. So weak models
> punch above their weight, and the work compounds instead of starting from zero.

### 2:50 — Close (back on the live page)

> Most agent systems stop at a document: a plan, a review, a verdict. Crescendo
> stops at a working product behind a public address, and proof that it works.
> Any system can hash what its agents said. Only one that ships has a live artifact
> to hash.
